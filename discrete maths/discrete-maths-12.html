Subproblem B

In a tree on 131313 vertices there are exactly 222 vertices of degree 666. Does it follow that there is a vertex of degree 222 in this tree?

In a tree, the number of edges is one less than the number of vertices. Sum of degrees of all n vertices is equal to (n−1)×2=(13−1)×2=24(n - 1)\times2=(13-1)\times2=24(n−1)×2=(13−1)×2=24

Two vertices should have their sum of degrees equal to 666, which leaves 24−6×2=1224-6\times2=1224−6×2=12 for 111111 vertices.

Each vertex has its degree equal to at least 111 (otherwise the vertex is isolated, which is impossible). After assigning degree of 111 to each vertex, per Dirichlet principle, the degree of at least one of the vertices (which are not equal to 666) should be at least 222, which implies that there is a vertex of degree 222 in the tree.
