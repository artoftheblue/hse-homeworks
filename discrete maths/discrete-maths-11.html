Problem 2

Find the minimum number of vertices in a graph, the sum of vertex degrees of which is equal to 262626.

Maximum degree of a vertex in a graph that consists of NNN vertices is N−1N-1N−1. Therefore, the graph at max may have 262=13\frac{26}{2}=13226​=13 edges: 13≤N(N−1)213 \leq \frac{N(N-1)}{2}13≤2N(N−1)​ for the minimum of at least N=6N=6N=6 vertices. A full graph for 666 vertices would have the sum of degrees equal to 6×5=306\times5=306×5=30. If we remove a single edge twice (descreasing degrees of two vertices by 111 twice), we get 30−2×2=2630-2\times2=2630−2×2=26. Thus, the answer is N=6N=6N=6.

Answer: 666

Accounting to rotational symmetry and identical cases when C1C_1C1​ is interchanged with C2C_2C2​, there are 5 total cases:

  • 2-3: If two squares have a common vertex or no common vertexes, the resulting figure is two separate squares.
